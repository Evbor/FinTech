</TABLE> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B>Intel and Tsinghua Unigroup Collaborate to Accelerate Development and Adoption of Intel-based Mobile Devices
</B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman" ALIGN="center"><B>Intel to Invest Up to RMB 9 Billion (US$1.5 Billion) in Semiconductor Business under Tsinghua Unigroup </B></P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">SANTA CLARA, Calif., Sept. 25, 2014 &#150; Intel Corporation and Tsinghua Unigroup Ltd. (&#147;Tsinghua Unigroup&#148;), an operating
subsidiary of Tsinghua Holdings Co., Ltd., a solely state-owned limited liability corporation funded by Tsinghua University in China, today jointly announced that both parties have signed a series of agreements. The purpose of the agreements is to
expand the product offerings and adoption for Intel-based mobile devices in China and worldwide by jointly developing Intel<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">&reg;</SUP> Architecture and communications-based solutions for mobile phones.
Intel also has agreed to invest up to RMB&nbsp;9&nbsp;billion (about US$1.5&nbsp;billion) for a minority stake of approximately 20 percent of the holding company under Tsinghua Unigroup which will own Spreadtrum Communications and RDA
Microelectronics, subject to regulatory approvals and other closing conditions. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Both Spreadtrum and RDA, controlled by Tsinghua Unigroup,
are leading fabless semiconductor companies in China, which develop mobile chipset platforms for smart phones, feature phones and other consumer electronics products, supporting 2G, 3G and 4G wireless communications standards.</P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">&#147;China is now the largest consumption market for smartphones and has the largest number of Internet users in the world,&#148; said Brian
Krzanich, Intel CEO. &#147;These agreements with Tsinghua Unigroup underscore Intel&#146;s 29-year-long history of investing in and working in China. This partnership will also enhance our ability to support a wider range of mobile customers in
China and the rest of the world by more quickly delivering a broader portfolio of Intel architecture and communications technology solutions.&#148; </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Mr.&nbsp;Zhao Weiguo, Chairman and President of Tsinghua Unigroup, added, &#147;It has become a national priority of China to grow its
semiconductor industry. The strategic collaboration between Tsinghua Unigroup and Intel ranges from design and development to marketing and equity investments, which demonstrate Intel&#146;s confidence in the Chinese market and strong commitment to
Chinese semiconductor industry, which will accelerate the technology development and further strengthen the competitiveness and market position of Chinese semiconductor companies.&#148; </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:Times New Roman">Under the terms of the agreement, Spreadtrum Communications, Inc. will jointly create and sell a family of Intel Architecture-based
system-on-chips (SoCs). Initial products will be available beginning in the second half of next year and will be Intel Architecture-based SoCs sold by both companies. </P>
