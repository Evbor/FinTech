      today reported first-quarter revenue of $10.3 billion. The company
      reported operating income of $3.4 billion, net income of $2.4 billion
      and EPS of 43 cents.

      the most compelling product line-up in our history,&#8221; said Paul Otellini,
      Intel president and CEO. &#8220;These leadership products combined with
      growing worldwide demand and continued outstanding execution resulted in
      Intel&#8217;s best first quarter ever. Looking forward, we&#8217;re optimistic about
      our business as Intel products are designed into a variety of new and
      exciting segments.&#8221;

    <p>
      <u>Q1 2010 Highlights (all comparisons sequential)</u>
    </p>
    <ul>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        PC Client Group revenue was flat, with record mobile microprocessor
        revenue.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Data Center Group revenue down 8 percent.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Other Intel Architecture group revenue down 9 percent.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Intel&#174; Atom&#8482; microprocessor and chipset revenue of $355 million was
        down 19 percent.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        The average selling price (ASP) for microprocessors was slightly up.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Excluding shipments of Intel Atom microprocessors, the ASP was
        approximately flat.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        R&amp;D plus MG&amp;A spending of $3.1 billion was higher than the company&#8217;s
        prior expectation.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        The effective tax rate was 29 percent, in-line with the company&#8217;s
        prior expectation.
      </li>
    </ul>
