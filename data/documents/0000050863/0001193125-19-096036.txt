<P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:ARIAL"><B><I>Appointment of Chief Financial Officer </I></B></P> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:ARIAL">The Board of Directors (the
&#147;Board&#148;) of Intel Corporation (&#147;Intel&#148;) appointed George&nbsp;S. Davis as Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer of Intel, effective as of April&nbsp;3, 2019 (the &#147;Effective Date&#148;). In this role, he will
oversee Intel&#146;s global finance and information technology organizations. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:ARIAL">Mr.&nbsp;Davis, 61, served as Executive Vice President and Chief Financial Officer of
QUALCOMM Incorporated, a global provider of wireless technologies, from March 2013 to April 2019, where he led the finance, information technology, and investor relations organizations. Prior to joining Qualcomm, Mr.&nbsp;Davis was Chief Financial
Officer of Applied Materials, Inc. from November 2006 to March 2013. Mr.&nbsp;Davis held several other leadership positions at Applied Materials from November 1999 to November 2006. Prior to joining Applied Materials, Mr.&nbsp;Davis served for 19
years with Atlantic Richfield Company in a number of finance and other corporate positions. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:ARIAL">In connection with his appointment as Chief Financial Officer, Intel
entered into an offer letter with Mr.&nbsp;Davis (the &#147;Offer Letter&#148;) setting forth the terms of his employment and compensation. Pursuant to the Offer Letter, Mr.&nbsp;Davis&#146; base salary will be $900,000, and he will be eligible for
an annual incentive cash bonus with a target amount of $1,485,000 under Intel&#146;s 2014 Annual Performance Bonus Plan and a quarterly incentive cash bonus under Intel&#146;s broad-based quarterly bonus program. Beginning in January 2020 and
subject to approval by the Board, he will be eligible to receive annual equity awards with an aggregate target grant date value of $6,500,000, which is expected to be comprised of 80% performance-based restricted stock units (&#147;PSUs&#148;) and
20% time-based restricted stock units (&#147;RSUs&#148;). </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:ARIAL">The Offer Letter provides for certain special equity awards to be granted to Mr.&nbsp;Davis in connection
with his appointment as Chief Financial Officer. These include PSUs (&#147;Special PSUs&#148;) for a target amount of 150,000 Intel shares, which will be earned based on the appreciation of Intel&#146;s closing stock price over a five-year period
following February&nbsp;1, 2019, the same performance period as similar equity awards granted to Intel&#146;s Chief Executive Officer and other senior leaders. The maximum number of Intel shares that may be earned under such Special PSUs is 300,000
shares. Mr.&nbsp;Davis also will be granted an option to purchase 600,000 Intel shares, with 25% vesting on the first anniversary of the grant date and the remaining 75% vesting ratably on February&nbsp;1, 2021, 2022 and 2023. The option, to the
extent vested, shall be exercisable only if, during the five-year period following February&nbsp;1, 2019 (the same performance period as similar equity awards granted to Intel&#146;s Chief Executive Officer and other senior leaders), Intel&#146;s
closing stock price trades at or above a threshold stock price for 30 consecutive trading days. The threshold stock price will be the greater of (i) 30% above Intel&#146;s closing stock price on February&nbsp;1, 2019, and (ii) 15% above Intel&#146;s
closing stock price on April&nbsp;2, 2019. The Offer Letter also provides that Mr.&nbsp;Davis will be granted RSUs with a target grant date value of $10,000,000, vesting quarterly over a three-year period from the grant date. The foregoing equity
grants will be subject to the terms and conditions of Intel&#146;s 2006 Equity Incentive Plan. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:ARIAL">In addition, Mr.&nbsp;Davis will receive a cash hiring bonus of
$4,000,000, payable in three installments: the first payment of $2,000,000 will be made within 30 days following the Effective Date, the second payment of $1,000,000 will be made on the first anniversary of the Effective Date, and the final payment
of $1,000,000 will be made on the second anniversary of the Effective Date. In the event his employment is terminated by Intel without &#147;Cause&#148; (as defined in the Offer Letter) or by him for &#147;Good Reason&#148; (as defined in the Offer
Letter) within the approximately <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">3-year</FONT> period after the Effective Date, Mr.&nbsp;Davis also will be entitled to receive any unpaid portion of his hiring bonus as well as a severance payment, the value of
which declines from $10,000,000 by 1/12<SUP STYLE="font-size:85%; vertical-align:top">th</SUP> each quarter over such three-year period, subject to his execution of an effective release of claims in favor of Intel. Mr.&nbsp;Davis also will be
provided with relocation services, including temporary living accommodations for up to 12 months. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:ARIAL">The foregoing description of Mr.&nbsp;Davis&#146;s compensation
arrangements is qualified in its entirety by reference to the Offer Letter, which is attached as Exhibit 10.1 to this report. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:ARIAL">Mr.&nbsp;Davis will also enter into
Intel&#146;s standard form of officers&#146; indemnification agreement with Intel, pursuant to which Intel agrees to indemnify its officers to the fullest extent permitted by applicable law and subject to certain conditions to advance expenses in
connection with proceedings as described in the indemnification agreement. </P>
