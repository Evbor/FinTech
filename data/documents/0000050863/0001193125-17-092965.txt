<TD ALIGN="left" VALIGN="top"><B>Election of New Directors</B> </TD></TR></TABLE> <P STYLE="margin-top:6pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:ARIAL">On March&nbsp;21, 2017, the Board of Directors (the &#147;Board&#148;) of Intel
Corporation (&#147;Intel&#148;) elected Omar Ishrak as a member of the Board, effective immediately, and elected Gregory D. Smith as a member of the Board, effective as of March&nbsp;23, 2017. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:ARIAL">The Board has determined that each of Mr.&nbsp;Ishrak and Mr.&nbsp;Smith qualifies as &#147;independent&#148; in accordance with the published listing requirements of
NASDAQ. Neither Mr.&nbsp;Ishrak nor Mr.&nbsp;Smith has been appointed to any Board committees at this time. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:ARIAL">Mr.&nbsp;Ishrak, 61, is the Chairman and Chief
Executive Officer of Medtronic plc, serving in that role since 2011. Prior to joining Medtronic, he spent 16 years in various roles with General Electric Company, most recently as President and Chief Executive Officer of GE Healthcare Systems, a
division of GE Healthcare. Mr.&nbsp;Ishrak is a member of the board of trustees of the Asia Society and a member of the board of directors of Minnesota Public Radio. He holds a Bachelor of Science degree and Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering from the
University of London, King&#146;s College. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:ARIAL">Mr.&nbsp;Smith, 50, is the Chief Financial Officer, Executive Vice President, Corporate Development and Strategy of The
Boeing Company, serving in that role since February 2015. Mr.&nbsp;Smith previously served as Boeing&#146;s Executive Vice President, Chief Financial Officer since February 2012, and he held various other leadership roles with Boeing since 2008.
Prior to that, he served for four years as Vice President of Global Investor Relations at Raytheon Company. Mr.&nbsp;Smith serves on the board of trustees for the Chicago Museum of Science and Industry, and on the board of directors of the Economic
Club of Chicago, the Chicago Botanic Garden, and the Northwestern Medicine Community Physicians Group. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:ARIAL">Mr.&nbsp;Ishrak and Mr.&nbsp;Smith will each receive the
standard compensation amounts payable to <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">non-employee</FONT> directors of the Board.&nbsp;Pursuant to these arrangements, commencing in March 2017, Mr.&nbsp;Ishrak and Mr.&nbsp;Smith will each be paid an annual
retainer of $90,000 (in addition to any committee fees), which will be <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">pro-rated</FONT> for their first year of service.&nbsp;In addition, in May 2017, Mr.&nbsp;Ishrak and Mr.&nbsp;Smith will each be granted <FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">non-employee</FONT> director outperformance restricted stock units and restricted stock units. Each director&#146;s awards will have a cumulative market value on the grant date of approximately $183,340, which is <FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">pro-rated</FONT> from the value of the annual awards granted to <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">non-employee</FONT> directors, and the awards will vest on the same schedule as the annual awards granted to <FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">non-employee</FONT> directors in February 2017, subject to each director&#146;s respective continued service on the Board. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:ARIAL">Mr.&nbsp;Ishrak and Mr.&nbsp;Smith will also each enter into Intel&#146;s standard form of directors&#146; indemnification agreement with Intel, pursuant to which Intel
agrees to indemnify its directors to the fullest extent permitted by applicable law and subject to certain conditions to advance expenses in connection with proceedings as described in the indemnification agreement. </P>

<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:ARIAL">SANTA CLARA, Calif., March&nbsp;23, 2017 &#150; Intel Corporation today announced that Omar Ishrak and Greg Smith have been elected to Intel&#146;s board of directors.
</P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:ARIAL">&#147;We are very pleased to welcome two new, independent directors with the depth of leadership experience at innovative, global companies that
both Mr.&nbsp;Ishrak and Mr.&nbsp;Smith bring,&#148; said Intel Chairman Andy Bryant. &#147;We look forward to their valuable contributions as Intel continues to transform itself for growth in emerging, adjacent market segments.&#148; </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:ARIAL">Ishrak, 61, is the chairman and chief executive officer of Medtronic, a global leader in medical technology. He has served in that role since 2011.
Prior to joining Medtronic, he spent 16 years in various roles with General Electric Company, most recently as president and chief executive officer of GE Healthcare Systems, a division of GE Healthcare. He is a member of the board of trustees of
the Asia Society, which promotes mutual understanding and strengthening partnerships among peoples, leaders and institutions of Asia and the United States in a global context, and a member of the board of directors for Minnesota Public Radio. </P>
<P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; text-indent:4%; font-size:10pt; font-family:ARIAL">Smith, 50, is the chief financial officer and executive vice president of Corporate Development and Strategy at Boeing, the world&#146;s largest
aerospace and defense company. He has served as Boeing&#146;s finance leader since 2012 and its strategy leader since 2015. Previously, Smith held various leadership roles across Boeing&#146;s finance function and operations. He rejoined Boeing in
2008 after serving for four years as vice president of Global Investor Relations at Raytheon. Smith serves on the board of trustees for the Chicago Museum of Science and Industry, and the board of directors of the Economic Club of Chicago, the
Chicago Botanic Garden, and the Northwestern Medicine Community Physicians Group. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:18pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:ARIAL"><B>About Intel </B></P>
