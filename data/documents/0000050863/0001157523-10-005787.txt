    <p>
      SANTA CLARA, Calif.--(BUSINESS WIRE)--October 12, 2010--Intel
      Corporation today reported that third-quarter revenue exceeded $11
      billion for the first time, up 18 percent year-over-year to $11.1
      billion. The company reported operating income of $4.1 billion, net
      income of $3.0 billion and EPS of 52 cents.
    </p>
    <p>
      &#8220;Intel&#8217;s third-quarter results set all-time records for revenue and
      operating income,&#8221; said Paul Otellini, Intel president and CEO. &#8220;These
      results were driven by solid demand from corporate customers, sales of
      our leadership products and continued growth in emerging markets.
      Looking forward, we continue to see healthy worldwide demand for
      computing products of all types and are particularly excited about our
      next-generation processor, codenamed Sandy Bridge, and the many new
      designs around our Intel&#174; Atom&#8482; processors in everything from the new
      Google TV* products to a wide array of tablets based on Windows*,
      Android* and MeeGo* operating systems.&#8221;
    </p>
    <p>
      <u>Q3 2010 Highlights</u>
    </p>
    <ul>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        PC Client Group revenue was up 3 percent sequentially, with record
        mobile microprocessor revenue.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Data Center Group revenue was up 3 percent sequentially, with record
        server microprocessor revenue.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Intel Atom microprocessor and chipset revenue of $396 million, down 4
        percent sequentially.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        The average selling price (ASP) for microprocessors was approximately
        flat sequentially and up significantly year-over-year.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Gross margin was 66 percent, consistent with the company&#8217;s revised
        expectation of 65 to 67 percent.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        R&amp;D plus MG&amp;A spending was $3.2 billion, consistent with the company&#8217;s
        expectation.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        The net gain from equity investments and interest and other was $115
        million, lower than the company&#8217;s revised expectation of $175 million.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        The effective tax rate was 30.5 percent, slightly below the company&#8217;s
        expectation of approximately 32 percent.
      </li>
    </ul>
