    <p style="text-align: center">
      <font style="font-family: Times New Roman; font-size: 12pt"><b>Intel
      Reports Full Year Revenue of $53.3 Billion, Net Income of $11.0 Billion</b></font>
    </p>
    <p style="text-align: center">
      <font style="font-family: Times New Roman; font-size: 12pt"><b>Generates
      $18.9 Billion in Cash from Operations</b></font>
    </p>
    <p>
      SANTA CLARA, Calif.--(BUSINESS WIRE)--January 17, 2013--Intel
      Corporation today reported full-year revenue of $53.3 billion, operating
      income of $14.6 billion, net income of $11.0 billion and EPS of $2.13.
      The company generated approximately $18.9 billion in cash from
      operations, paid dividends of $4.4 billion, and used $4.8 billion to
      repurchase 191 million shares of stock.
    </p>
    <p>
      For the fourth quarter, Intel posted revenue of $13.5 billion, operating
      income of $3.2 billion, net income of $2.5 billion and EPS of 48 cents.
      The company generated approximately $6 billion in cash from operations,
      paid dividends of $1.1 billion and used $1.0 billion to repurchase 47
      million shares of stock.
    </p>
    <p>
      &#8220;The fourth quarter played out largely as expected as we continued to
      execute through a challenging environment,&#8221; said Paul Otellini, Intel
      president and CEO. &#8220;We made tremendous progress across the business in
      2012 as we entered the market for smartphones and tablets, worked with
      our partners to reinvent the PC, and drove continued innovation and
      growth in the data center. As we enter 2013, our strong product pipeline
      has us well positioned to bring a new wave of Intel innovations across
      the spectrum of computing.&#8221;
    </p>
    <p>
      <u>Full-Year 2012 Key Financial Information and Business Unit Trends</u>
    </p>
    <ul>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        PC Client Group had revenue of $34.3 billion, down 3 percent from 2011.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Data Center Group had revenue of $10.7 billion, up 6 percent from 2011.
      </li>
