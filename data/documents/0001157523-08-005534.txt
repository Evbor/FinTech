      record second-quarter revenue of $9.5 billion, operating income of $2.3
      billion, net income of $1.6 billion and earnings per share (EPS) of 28
      cents.

      expectations and earnings up substantially year over year,&#8221; said Paul
      Otellini, Intel president and CEO. &#8220;As we enter the second half, demand

      segments and all parts of the globe.&#8221;

    </p>
    <ul>

        Mobile microprocessor and chipset units both set records.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Total microprocessor units were up sequentially and higher than
        seasonal.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Gross margin of 55.4 percent was up from 53.8 percent in the first
        quarter and slightly below the midpoint of the previous expectation as
        growth in demand for lower-priced notebook PCs resulted in a lower
        than expected microprocessor average selling price.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Restructuring and asset impairment charges of $96 million were lower
        than the previous expectation of approximately $250 million.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        The effective tax rate for the quarter was 31 percent, lower than the
        previous expectation of approximately 33 percent due to a tax
        settlement.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        The company used $2.5 billion to repurchase 109 million shares of its
        common stock.
