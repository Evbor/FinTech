      <font style="font-family: Times New Roman; font-size: 12pt"><b>Intel
      Reports Second-Quarter Revenue of $12.8 Billion, Net Income of $2.0
      Billion</b></font>
    </p>
    <ul>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        <font style="font-family: Times New Roman; font-size: 12pt"><b>4th
        Generation Intel&#174; Core&#8482; processor and Silvermont architecture unveiled
        to strong acceptance</b></font>
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        <font style="font-family: Times New Roman; font-size: 12pt"><b>New
        Intel&#174; Atom&#8482; processor and Intel LTE solution tapped for Samsung*
        GALAXY Tab-3 10.1-inch</b></font>
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        <font style="font-family: Times New Roman; font-size: 12pt"><b>New
        leadership team implemented one of the most significant management
        reorganizations in a decade</b></font>
      </li>
    </ul>
    <p>
      SANTA CLARA, Calif.--(BUSINESS WIRE)--July 17, 2013--Intel Corporation
      today reported second-quarter revenue of $12.8 billion, operating income
      of $2.7 billion, net income of $2.0 billion and EPS of $0.39. The
      company generated approximately $4.7 billion in cash from operations,
      paid dividends of $1.1 billion, and used $550 million to repurchase 23
      million shares of stock.
    </p>
    <p>
      &#8220;In the second quarter, we delivered on our quarterly outlook and made
      several key product announcements,&#8221; said Intel CEO Brian Krzanich. &#8220;In
      my first two months as CEO, I have listened to a wide variety of views
      about Intel and our industry from customers, employees and my leadership
      team and I am more confident than ever about our opportunity as a
      company.&#8221;
    </p>
    <p>
      &#8220;Looking ahead, the market will continue buying a wide range of
      computing products,&#8221; he added. &#8220;Intel Atom and Core processors and
      increased SOC integration will be Intel's future. We will leave no
      computing opportunity untapped. To embrace these opportunities, I've
      made it Intel's highest priority to create the best products for the
      fast growing ultra-mobile market segment.&#8221;
    </p>
    <p>
      <u>Q2 Key Financial Information and Business Unit Trends</u>
    </p>
    <ul>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        PC Client Group revenue of $8.1 billion, up 1.4 percent sequentially
        and down 7.5 percent year-over-year.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Data Center Group revenue of $2.7 billion, up 6.1 percent sequentially
        and flat year-over-year.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Other Intel&#174; Architecture Group revenue of $942 million, down 3.7
        percent sequentially and down 15.0 percent year-over-year.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Gross margin of 58 percent, up 2 percentage points sequentially and
        down 5 percentage points year-over-year.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        R&amp;D plus MG&amp;A spending of $4.7 billion, in line with the company&#8217;s
        expectation of approximately $4.7 billion.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Tax rate of 26 percent.
      </li>
    </ul>

      <u>Business Outlook</u>
    </p>
    <p>
      Intel&#8217;s Business Outlook does not include the potential impact of any
      business combinations, asset acquisitions, divestitures or other
      investments that may be completed after July&#160;17.
    </p>
    <p>
      <u>Q3 2013</u>
    </p>
    <ul>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Revenue: $13.5 billion, plus or minus $500 million.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Gross margin percentage: 61 percent, plus or minus a couple of
        percentage points.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        R&amp;D plus MG&amp;A spending: approximately $4.8 billion.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Amortization of acquisition-related intangibles: approximately $70
        million.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Impact of equity investments and interest and other: approximately
        $400 million net gain.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Depreciation: approximately $1.7 billion.
      </li>
    </ul>
    <p>
      <u>Full-Year 2013</u>
    </p>
    <ul>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Revenue: Approximately flat year-on-year, down from prior expectations
        of low single digit percentage increase.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Gross margin percentage: 59 percent, plus or minus a couple percentage
        points, down from prior expectations of 60 percent, plus or minus a
        few percentage points.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        R&amp;D plus MG&amp;A spending: $18.7 billion, plus or minus $200 million,
        down $200 million from prior expectations.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Amortization of acquisition-related intangibles: approximately $300
        million, unchanged from prior expectations.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Depreciation: $6.8 billion, plus or minus $100 million, unchanged from
        prior expectations.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Tax Rate: approximately 26 percent for each of the remaining quarters
        of the year.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Full-year capital spending: $11.0 billion, plus or minus $500 million,
        down $1.0 billion from prior expectations.
      </li>
    </ul>
