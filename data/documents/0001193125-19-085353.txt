<TD ALIGN="left" VALIGN="top"> <P STYLE=" margin-top:0pt ; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:ARIAL; " ALIGN="left"><B>Departure of Directors or Certain Officers; Election of Directors; Appointment of Certain Officers; Compensatory
Arrangements of Certain Officers. </B></P></TD></TR></TABLE> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:ARIAL">On March&nbsp;22, 2019, the Board of Directors (the &#147;Board&#148;) of Intel Corporation
(&#147;Intel&#148;) unanimously waived the provision contained in Article&nbsp;III, Section&nbsp;12 of Intel&#146;s Bylaws that limits the tenure of the Chairman of the Board to no more than two three-year terms, in order to allow Andy D. Bryant,
Intel&#146;s current Chairman, to serve for an additional <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">one-year</FONT> term. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:ARIAL">If Mr.&nbsp;Bryant is <FONT
STYLE="white-space:nowrap">re-elected</FONT> to the Board by stockholders at Intel&#146;s 2019 Annual Stockholders&#146; Meeting, he would be permitted to continue serving as Chairman through the conclusion of Intel&#146;s 2020 Annual
Stockholders&#146; Meeting. Mr.&nbsp;Bryant indicated to the Board on March&nbsp;22, 2019 that, if <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">re-elected,</FONT> he would not expect to stand for <FONT STYLE="white-space:nowrap">re-election</FONT> again at
Intel&#146;s 2020 Annual Stockholders&#146; Meeting. </P> <P STYLE="margin-top:12pt; margin-bottom:0pt; font-size:10pt; font-family:ARIAL">The Board approved the waiver of the Chairman term limit for one additional year because it believes
Mr.&nbsp;Bryant&#146;s extensive expertise and experience provide important leadership continuity, particularly to help support Intel&#146;s new CEO, Robert H. Swan, during a time of substantial business transformation. </P>

senior executive positions, including as chief financial officer and chief administrative officer and has served as Intel&#146;s chairman for the past 7 years. </P>
