    <p style="text-align: center">
      <font style="font-size: 12pt; font-family: Times New Roman"><b>Intel
      Reports Third-Quarter Revenue of $13.5 Billion</b></font>
    </p>
    <p>
      SANTA CLARA, Calif.--(BUSINESS WIRE)--October 16, 2012--Intel
      Corporation today reported quarterly revenue of $13.5 billion, operating
      income of $3.8 billion, net income of $3.0 billion and EPS of $0.58. The
      company generated approximately $5.1 billion in cash from operations,
      paid dividends of $1.1 billion and used $1.2 billion to repurchase stock.
    </p>
    <p>
      &quot;Our third-quarter results reflected a continuing tough economic
      environment,&quot; said Paul Otellini, Intel president and CEO. &quot;The world of
      computing is in the midst of a period of breakthrough innovation and
      creativity. As we look to the fourth quarter, we're pleased with the
      continued progress in Ultrabooks and phones and excited about the range
      of Intel-based tablets coming to market.&quot;
    </p>
    <p>
      <u>Q3 2012 Key Financial Information and Business Unit Trends (GAAP,
      unless otherwise stated)</u>
    </p>
    <ul>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        PC Client Group revenue of $8.6 billion, flat sequentially and down 8
        percent year-over-year
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Data Center Group revenue of $2.7 billion, down 5 percent sequentially
        and up 6 percent year-over-year
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Other Intel&#174; architecture group revenue of $1.2 billion, up 6 percent
        sequentially and down 14 percent year-over-year
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Gross margin of 63.3 percent, 1.3 percentage points above the midpoint
        of the company&#8217;s updated expectation of 62 percent.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        R&amp;D plus MG&amp;A spending $4.6 billion, unchanged.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Tax rate of 24 percent, below the company&#8217;s expectation of
        approximately 28 percent.
      </li>
    </ul>
    <p>
      <u>Business Outlook</u>
    </p>
    <p>
      Intel&#8217;s Business Outlook does not include the potential impact of any
      business combinations, asset acquisitions, divestitures or other
      investments that may be completed after Oct. 16.
    </p>
    <p>
      <u>Q4 2012 (GAAP, unless otherwise stated)</u>
    </p>
    <ul>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Revenue: $13.6 billion, plus or minus $500 million.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Gross margin percentage: 57 percent and 58 percent Non-GAAP (excluding
        amortization of acquisition-related intangibles), both plus or minus a
        couple of percentage points.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        R&amp;D plus MG&amp;A spending: approximately $4.5 billion.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Amortization of acquisition-related intangibles: approximately $75
        million.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Impact of equity investments and interest and other: approximately $75
        million.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Depreciation: approximately $1.6 billion.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Tax Rate: approximately 27 percent.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Full-year capital spending: $11.3 billion, plus or minus $300 million.
      </li>
    </ul>
    <p>
      For additional information regarding Intel&#8217;s results and Business
      Outlook, please see the CFO commentary at: <u>www.intc.com/results.cfm</u>.
