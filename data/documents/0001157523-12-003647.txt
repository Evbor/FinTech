    <p style="text-align: center">
      <font style="font-family: Times New Roman; font-size: 12pt"><b>Intel
      Reports Second-Quarter Revenue of $13.5 Billion</b></font>
    </p>
    <p>
      SANTA CLARA, Calif.--(BUSINESS WIRE)--July 17, 2012--Intel Corporation
      today reported quarterly revenue of $13.5 billion, operating income of
      $3.8 billion, net income of $2.8 billion and EPS of $0.54. The company
      generated approximately $4.7 billion in cash from operations, paid
      dividends of $1.1 billion and used $1.1 billion to repurchase stock.
    </p>
    <p>
      &#8220;The second quarter was highlighted by solid execution with continued
      strength in the data center and multiple product introductions in
      Ultrabooks and smartphones,&#8221; said Paul Otellini, Intel president and
      CEO. &#8220;As we enter the third quarter, our growth will be slower than we
      anticipated due to a more challenging macroeconomic environment.&#160;With a
      rich mix of Ultrabook and Intel-based tablet&#160;and phone&#160;introductions in
      the second half,&#160;combined with the&#160;long-term investments we're making
      in&#160;our&#160;product and manufacturing areas, we are well positioned for this
      year and beyond.&#8221;
    </p>
    <p>
      <u>Business Outlook</u>
    </p>
    <p>
      Intel&#8217;s Business Outlook does not include the potential impact of any
      business combinations, asset acquisitions, divestitures or other
      investments that may be completed after July 17.
    </p>
    <p>
      <u>Q3 2012 (GAAP, unless otherwise stated)</u>
    </p>
    <ul>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Revenue: $14.3 billion, plus or minus $500 million.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Gross margin percentage: 63 percent and 64 percent Non-GAAP (excluding
        amortization of acquisition-related intangibles), both plus or minus a
        couple of percentage points.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        R&amp;D plus MG&amp;A spending: approximately $4.6 billion.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Amortization of acquisition-related intangibles: approximately $80
        million.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Impact of equity investments and interest and other: approximately
        zero.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Depreciation: approximately $1.6 billion.
      </li>
    </ul>
    <p>
      <u>Full-Year 2012 (GAAP, unless otherwise stated)</u>
    </p>
    <ul>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Revenue up between 3 percent and 5 percent year over year, down from
        the prior expectation for high single-digit growth.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Gross margin percentage: 64 percent and 65 percent Non-GAAP (excluding
        amortization of acquisition-related intangibles), both plus or minus a
        couple points.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Spending (R&amp;D plus MG&amp;A): $18.2 billion, plus or minus $200 million,
        down $100 million from prior expectations.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Amortization of acquisition-related intangibles: approximately $300
        million, unchanged.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Depreciation: $6.3 billion, plus or minus $100 million, down $100
        million from prior expectations.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Tax Rate: approximately 28 percent, unchanged.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Full-year capital spending: $12.5 billion, plus or minus $400 million,
        unchanged.
      </li>
    </ul>
    <p>
      For additional information regarding Intel&#8217;s results and Business
      Outlook, please see the CFO commentary at: <u>www.intc.com/results.cfm</u>.
