    <p>
      SANTA CLARA, Calif.--(BUSINESS WIRE)--January 13, 2011--Intel
      Corporation today reported full-year revenue of $43.6 billion, operating
      income of $15.9 billion, net income of $11.7 billion, and EPS of $2.05 &#8211;
      all records. The company generated approximately $16.7 billion in cash
      from operations, paid cash dividends of $3.5 billion, and used $1.5
      billion to repurchase 70 million shares of common stock.
    </p>
    <p>
      For the fourth-quarter, Intel posted revenue of $11.5 billion. The
      company reported fourth-quarter operating income of $4.3 billion, net
      income of $3.4 billion, and EPS of 59 cents. Fourth-quarter revenue,
      operating income, net income, and EPS were also all records.
    </p>
    <p>
      &#8220;2010 was the best year in Intel&#8217;s history. We believe that 2011 will be
      even better,&#8221; said Paul Otellini, Intel president and CEO.

        percent, other Intel architecture group revenue up 27 percent, and
        Intel&#174; Atom&#8482; microprocessor and chipset revenue of $1.6 billion up 8
        percent.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Gross margin of 66 percent, up 10 percentage points compared to 2009.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Full-year capital spending was $5.2 billion, consistent with the
        company&#8217;s expectation.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        The company used $1.5 billion to repurchase 70 million shares of
        common stock.
      </li>
    </ul>
    <p>
      <u>Q4 2010 Key Financial Information</u>
    </p>
    <ul>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        PC Client Group revenue flat, Data Center Group revenue up 15 percent,
        other Intel architecture group flat, and Intel Atom microprocessor and
        chipset revenue of $391 million flat, all sequentially.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        The average selling price (ASP) for microprocessors was slightly up
        sequentially.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        Gross margin was 67.5 percent, slightly above the company&#8217;s
        expectation.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        R&amp;D plus MG&amp;A spending of $3.4 billion was higher than the company&#8217;s
        expectation.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        The net gain of $140 million from equity investments and interest and
        other was better than the company&#8217;s expectation.
      </li>
      <li style="margin-bottom: 10.0px">
        The effective tax rate was 24 percent, lower than the company&#8217;s
        expectation of 31 percent primarily due to the retroactive
        reinstatement of the U.S. R&amp;D tax credit.
      </li>
    </ul>
